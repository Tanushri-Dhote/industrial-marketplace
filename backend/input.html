A Brief History of Hyundai

Hyundai Motor Company is a multinational automaker founded by Chung Ju-Yung. It was founded in 1947 initially as a construction company. With the help of Korean government, they (Chung Ju-yung and family) swiftly dominated into number of businesses, eventually becoming South Korea's second biggest 'Chaebol'.

After Asian financial crisis in 1997, Hyundai started and spun off many better known businesses such as Hyundai Automotive Group, Hyundai Department Stores Group and Hyundai Heavy Industries Group. Presently, the focus point of the Hyundai group is on container services and elevators along with the tourism to mount Kumgang.

Hyundai Santa Fe

The Hyundai Santa Fe is a SUV produced by the South Korean manufacturer Hyundai since 2000. It is named after the city of Santa Fe, New Mexico, and was introduced in 2001 as Hyundai's first SUV. It is in its sixth generation now and is sold in bug numbers all over the world.
